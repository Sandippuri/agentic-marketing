GTM Plan: Marketing Agent SaaS — First 90 Days
Context
You're a solo developer launching a marketing-agent SaaS at $25 / $99 / $299 tiers. You need paying customers fast (next 90 days) without burning capital, and you've committed to 10-15 hrs/week of marketing time, text + screenshots only (no video), targeting B2B SaaS founders + small marketing agencies.

The strategy below picks one primary channel (LinkedIn) instead of spraying everywhere, because a solo dev's only scalable advantage is depth in one place — being the 1000th person on TikTok beats nothing, but being the 5th most-followed AI marketing founder on LinkedIn-for-SaaS gets you 50 inbound DMs/week.

The intended outcome: 30-50 paying customers and $2-4K MRR by Day 90, with a repeatable channel that keeps producing leads after launch noise dies.

The single most important decision: niche down at launch
You can serve BOTH SaaS founders AND agencies, but you can only launch with one positioning. The website, every LinkedIn post, every DM has to speak to ONE buyer or it speaks to none.

Pick ICP 1 (B2B SaaS founders) as the launch wedge. Reasons:

They live on LinkedIn (your channel) — agencies are on LinkedIn too but less actively
They're early adopters of AI tools (agencies are conservative buyers)
The Pro tier ($99) is the sweet spot for both, but SaaS founders convert in days; agencies take weeks
You ARE one — credibility is free
Agencies will naturally find you in month 3-6 once SaaS founders refer them
Launch positioning: "AI marketing team for B2B SaaS founders. Stop spending 10 hrs/week writing LinkedIn posts."

Once you have 30+ SaaS founder customers, expand to agencies with a separate landing page and positioning.

Channel allocation (10-15 hrs/week)
Channel Hours/week Why
LinkedIn (primary) 7-9 hrs ICP lives here. Text+screenshots is native format. You can dogfood (use the tool to post about the tool).
X/Twitter (secondary) 2-3 hrs #buildinpublic community converts SaaS founders. Lower effort once LinkedIn posts are written — repurpose.
Reddit + Indie Hackers 1-2 hrs Free traffic, takes weeks to compound. Answer questions, never promote.
Direct outreach 1-2 hrs 25 hand-picked DMs/week. Highest conversion, doesn't scale, but bootstraps proof.
Skip everything else for 90 days: no SEO, no paid ads, no Product Hunt yet (save for Day 60), no podcast, no YouTube. Saying no to channels is how solo devs win.

The 90-day sequence
Days 1-14 — Foundation
Goal: Set up the machine. No customers yet, that's fine.

LinkedIn profile rebuild: headline = "Building [Product]: AI marketing team for B2B SaaS founders | $99/mo replaces a $3K/mo agency", about section frames the journey, banner image
Landing page at the marketing-agent product itself (/): clear ICP statement, 3-tier pricing, 14-day free trial, no-credit-card signup, single CTA above the fold
Waitlist page for non-converters with email capture
Onboarding flow that gets a new user to their first AI-generated post in <5 minutes (this is where they decide to keep paying)
Write 10 LinkedIn posts queued in Buffer — narrative posts about building the product, with screenshots from inside the app
Define one specific outcome metric to feature publicly: "I'll be at $X MRR by Day Y" — accountability drives engagement
Set up cost-cap enforcement (the LLM $ caps we spec'd) — without this, free trials will bleed you
Days 15-30 — Soft launch (waitlist mode)
Goal: 200 waitlist signups, 5-10 design partners.

Post daily on LinkedIn (Mon-Fri). Each post follows one of:
Build-in-public: "Day X of building [product]. This week I shipped Y because customers said Z." + screenshot
Teardown: "Here's how I'd rewrite [competitor]'s LinkedIn post in 30 seconds with AI." + side-by-side
Founder pain: "If you're a SaaS founder spending 10 hrs/week on LinkedIn content, here's what I learned about agencies." + actual numbers
DM 10 dream customers/day for waitlist. Template: "Hey [name], saw your post about [specific thing they posted]. I'm building an AI marketing tool for SaaS founders specifically — would love 10 min of feedback in exchange for free Pro access. No pitch."
Cross-post the best LinkedIn posts to X as 4-5 tweet threads. Tag #buildinpublic.
Indie Hackers milestone post: "I'm a solo dev building an AI marketing SaaS — here's why"
Find 5 design partners from waitlist DMs — give them free Pro access for 6 months in exchange for: weekly 30-min feedback call, a written testimonial on Day 60, permission to use their name/logo
Days 31-60 — Public launch + first paying customers
Goal: 30 paying customers, $1.5-2.5K MRR.

Product Hunt launch on a Tuesday (12:01am PST). Prep 2 weeks ahead: hunter lined up, 30 supporters notified, gallery images using the product, founder comment with the origin story
Launch discount: PH50 code = 50% off first 3 months on Pro/Business (encourages cards on file)
r/SideProject + r/SaaS post: "I built an AI marketing tool for SaaS founders — here's what I learned about pricing/COGS"
Email the waitlist with launch + discount (this is your highest-converting moment of the year)
Hacker News "Show HN" with a specific technical angle (e.g. "Show HN: How I cut image-gen costs 60% with a single-shot pipeline + retry") — front-page traffic if the title delivers
Convert design partners to paid at full price after their 6 months OR earlier if they're getting value
Continue daily LinkedIn posts — now mixing in customer wins ("Day 32 customer just got a 5K-view post out of my tool, here's the prompt")
Days 61-90 — Scale the winners
Goal: 75-100 paying customers, $3-5K MRR.

Identify the top-performing channel (likely LinkedIn) and shift 80% of marketing time there
Affiliate / referral program: 30% recurring commission for 12 months. Promote inside the app + on LinkedIn
Case studies: 3 written case studies from design partners (use the actual tool to write them)
Expand to ICP 2 (agencies) with a separate landing page /agencies + a different LinkedIn post series targeting agency owners
Start answering on Reddit with depth — top comment on 1-2 marketing/SaaS threads per week. Long compounding play.
Founder office hours: 2× per week, 30 min slots, open booking link. Free advice → trust → upsell
Critical messaging primitives
One-liner (use everywhere)
"AI marketing team for B2B SaaS founders. $99/mo replaces a $3K/mo agency."

Three pain hooks (rotate across LinkedIn posts)
Time: "If you're a SaaS founder spending 10+ hrs/week on LinkedIn content, you're trading $200/hr engineering time for $40/hr writing."
Money: "Marketing agencies charge $2-3K/mo for what an AI agent does for $99/mo. I built the agent."
Quality: "AI content is bad because it has no brand context. This one reads your past wins and writes in your voice."
Three proof formats (collect these aggressively)
Screenshots of the tool generating a real post for a real customer
Before/after: "Customer's old LinkedIn post (8 likes) → same topic via the tool (340 likes)"
Founder transparency: "Here's my exact COGS and pricing math" (people love this on X/IH)
What NOT to do (solo dev traps)
Don't launch on Product Hunt in week 1. You'll get 100 signups, 0 buyers, and burn your one big launch on an unpolished product. Wait until Day 50+.
Don't run paid ads in the first 90 days. Until you know your conversion rate from organic, every $1 spent on ads is a guess. Solo devs lose more to bad CAC than to slow organic.
Don't build features customers haven't asked for. Every feature you add when you have <20 paying customers is a guess. Spend the time on marketing instead.
Don't price-discount past 50% off. Lifetime deals (AppSumo) on a usage-based product = guaranteed loss when 5% of buyers max out their plan forever. Avoid.
Don't try to serve everyone. Resist the urge to put ecommerce, creators, and local biz on the landing page in month 1. SaaS founders ONLY. Niche compounds.

If this plan doesn't fit — branches to consider
You only have <5 hrs/week: Skip LinkedIn daily posting. Focus on hand-curated cold DMs (10/week) + one big launch event (Product Hunt at Day 30). Lower ceiling but lower time cost.
You're willing to spend $500-1K on ads: Run LinkedIn ads to the SaaS-founder ICP, $20/day retargeting waitlist signups. Doubles speed of customer #1-30.
You decide to start with agencies instead of SaaS founders: Reduces LinkedIn-organic effectiveness (agencies are quieter), increases reliance on direct outreach and Facebook agency groups. Higher LTV but slower start.
