## Single post vs. thread

- **Single post**: Opinion, hook, quick insight. 240–280 characters (leave room for engagement).
- **Thread**: Step-by-step explanation, story, listicle. 5–10 tweets sweet spot. First tweet must be compelling as a standalone.

## Thread structure

1. **Tweet 1** (hook): State the payoff upfront. "How we X'd Y in Z days. A thread:" — or use a strong question/provocative claim. This is your headline.
2. **Tweets 2–N** (body): One idea per tweet. No filler. Short paragraphs or numbered bullets.
3. **Final tweet** (CTA + link): "If this was useful, follow for more. Full post + resources: [link]" — links only in the last tweet for maximum feed reach.

## Character and format rules

- 280 characters per tweet.
- **Links suppress reach** — put external links in the last tweet or first reply only.
- **Images**: 16:9 (1600×900) for single image; up to 4 images per tweet.
- **Alt text** on every image.
- Threads perform significantly better when kicked off with a quoted image on tweet 1.

## Voice on X

- Shorter, more direct than LinkedIn. Opinions are welcome.
- Wit > formality. But never at the expense of clarity.
- First-person preferred over brand-speak.

## Hashtags

- X hashtags are declining in algorithmic value. Use 0–2 highly specific ones or none.
- Never stuff hashtags at the end — integrate or omit.

## Engagement

- Reply to quote-tweets and comments within 2 hours of posting.
- Pin high-performing threads to the profile for 30 days.
