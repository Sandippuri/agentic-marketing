## Format constraints

- **Post body**: 1,300 characters hard limit visible without "see more"; ideal 700–900.
- **Hook (first 1–2 lines)**: the only thing most people read — must earn the click to expand.
- **No links in the body copy**: LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with external links. Put the link in the first comment instead.
- **Hashtags**: 3–5 max, at the end. Use relevant niche tags over broad ones (e.g. `#b2bmarketing` over `#marketing`).

## Structure by stage

- **pull** (problem-awareness): Open with a relatable frustration. Scene-setting > statistics. End with a question that makes the reader want to read the answer.
- **explain** (mechanism): Short intro → numbered list or short paragraphs → one concrete example. Lists perform 30–40% better on LinkedIn than dense paragraphs.
- **reinforce** (position): Personal story or strong opinion. "I used to think X. I was wrong." Works well as a short carousel or single post with a contrarian hook.
- **push** (CTA): Direct. One ask. State the outcome, not the feature: "Book a 20-min call to see your publishing schedule for Q2" not "Try our product".

## Image guidance

- Aspect ratio: 1.91:1 (1200×628) for feed posts; 1:1 (1080×1080) for carousel covers.
- Text overlay: max 20% of image area (LinkedIn algorithm preference).
- Alt text: always set for accessibility.

- Reposts from the company page should happen 30–60 min after the personal post goes live.
